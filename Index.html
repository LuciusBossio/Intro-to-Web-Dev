Organic Cannabis Pest Management

Overview

Growing organic cannabis limits the strategies and or products we can utilize in our pest management program. However, as cannabis is a product that will be consumed by the end user, unlike ornamentals, organic pest management practices will ensure we produce the healthiest product possible. If organized correctly organic pest management can keep your garden free of all pests and will put less stress on your plants, which should result in greater product quality and yield.

Introducing Integrated Pest Management

Integrated Pest Management is a common method for controlling pests in the garden organically. Within IPM overall plant health is believed to be the best method for preventing and controlling pests, which involves creating a detailed program that integrates numerous strategies spanning a plantâ€™s life cycle. As cannabis pests are common and diverse, this is by far the most effective form of pest management. Strategies include the use of organic enzymes, predatory bugs, bacteria, mold, and funghi, organic fertilizers, and soil amendments.

Common Pests:

    • Root Aphids
    • Winged Aphids

  2. Mites
    • Spider Mites

    • Sooty Mold

  4. Hard Bodied Insects
    • Caterpillars
    • Grasshoppers
  5. Flying Insects
    • Whiteflies

  6. Viruses
    • Hops Latent Viroid
    • Tobacco Mosaic Virus
